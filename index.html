CASE STUDY 01 · PASSPORT GLOBAL · 2023–2025
Enterprise-Scale HS Code Classification Program
Global E-Commerce · NA · EU · UK · AU · APAC · Canada · Japan
100K+ SKUs Classified
99%+ Accuracy
35% Fewer Disputes
The Challenge

A top-tier global e-commerce client had a rapidly expanding product catalogue spanning hundreds of thousands of SKUs across electronics, apparel, cosmetics, and household goods. The existing classification process was inconsistent, manual, and error-prone — exposing the client to duty miscalculations, customs delays, and regulatory penalties across six international markets.

Actions Taken
  • Conducted a full audit of existing SKU classification records, identifying error patterns across country-specific tariff schedules
  • Built a classification framework using Descartes CustomsInfo and HS reference databases, creating product-type classification trees
  • Developed a tiered SKU triage system: batch processing for high-volume items, deep review escalation for borderline classifications
  • Created a master classification reference document reducing repeat lookups across the team
  • Maintained ongoing review cycles to update classifications for US Section 301 changes and UK Global Tariff post-Brexit
Tools Used
Descartes CustomsInfo Advanced Excel CargoWise One Salesforce Google Workspace
Business Impact

The classification program eliminated duty misclassification risks across six markets, reduced reclassification disputes by ~35%, and established a scalable reference system that continues to support the client's expanding product catalogue. Compliance accuracy maintained at 99%+ throughout the engagement.

CASE STUDY 02 · PASSPORT GLOBAL · 2023–2025
Clearance Bottleneck Resolution & On-Time Delivery Improvement
Multi-Region E-Commerce · Cross-Border Freight Optimization
15–20% OTD Improvement
30% Fewer Doc Errors
-2 Days Avg Clearance
The Challenge

Recurring customs clearance bottlenecks were causing delayed deliveries across multiple destination markets. Root causes included inconsistent documentation from origin partners, classification disputes at destination ports, and poor real-time visibility into in-transit shipment status — eroding client SLA performance and increasing cost-per-shipment.

Actions Taken
  • Implemented proactive shipment tracking protocols — monitoring at each customs checkpoint rather than waiting for exception alerts
  • Created a documentation checklist by origin country, eliminating the most common submission errors causing customs holds
  • Established direct communication channels with overseas logistics partners for real-time status updates
  • Developed weekly clearance performance KPI reports tracking hold rates, average clearance time, and country-level trends
  • Implemented pre-shipment compliance review for all high-value, high-volume lanes — catching errors before departure
Tools Used
CargoWise One Salesforce Advanced Excel Zendesk Google Workspace
Business Impact

On-time delivery rates improved 15–20% quarter-over-quarter. Documentation errors fell ~30%. Average customs clearance time dropped by 2 business days. The pre-shipment review process became a standard client protocol, preventing recurrence of the bottleneck pattern.
